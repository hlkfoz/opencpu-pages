OpenCPU is part of the (ongoing) PhD research on embedded scientific computing from Jeroen Ooms at the UCLA Department of Statistics. Advisers/committee members include Mark Hansen (Columbia School of Journalism), Jan de Leeuw (UCLA Statistics), Deborah Estrin (Cornell Tech) and Mark Handcock (UCLA Statistics).


The research focuses on several domain specific challenges related to integrating scientific computing into the next generation of systems and applications. Below some reading material that could be of interest to anyone who wishes to build such software (with or without OpenCPU).

Directions for Improved Dependency Versioning in R and CRAN

Ooms, Jeroen. The R Journal, Volume 5/1, June 2013.
Discusses management of the decentralized open source development process in the context of statistical software and specifically R. Explains how proper dependency management is key to reliable applications, stable repositories and reproducible results.

The RAppArmor Package: Enforcing Security Policies in R Using Dynamic Sandboxing on Linux

Ooms, Jeroen. Forthcoming in Journal of Statistical Software, 2013.
Discusses security and allocation of hardware resources in the context of computing services. Explains why standard approaches to security might not work for statistical software. Introduces a package to enforce security and hardware restrictions using mandatory access control and rlimit on Linux.

Open Computing: Challenges and Opportunities Proposal Draft.

PhD proposal draft from 2011 which outlined some of the initial ideas that led to OpenCPU. A lot has changed since then, but the overall idea still stands.
